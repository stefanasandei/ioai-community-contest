# Local Validation & Leaderboards

Your local validation score is your compass. If it does not correlate with the leaderboard, you are flying blind.

## Why validation matters

The public leaderboard is based on only a subset of test data. Overfitting it is easy and dangerous. A good local validation setup tells you whether your model is actually improving.

## Key techniques

- **Stratified k-fold:** preserve class proportions in every fold.
- **Time-based splits:** use past data to predict future data when time is involved.
- **Group splits:** keep related samples together to avoid leakage.
- **Cross-validation ensembling:** train multiple models and average predictions.

## Leaderboard strategy

- Trust your CV more than small leaderboard movements.
- Watch for shake-up between public and private leaderboards.
- Don't chase a single public submission with excessive tuning.

> Consistent local validation is the closest thing to a crystal ball in competitions.
