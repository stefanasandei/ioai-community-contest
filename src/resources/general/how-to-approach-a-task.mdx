# How to Approach a Task

Jumping straight into a complex model is one of the most common mistakes in AI competitions. A disciplined workflow will save you time and improve your results.

## Recommended workflow

1. **Read the task carefully.** Understand the input, output, metric, and constraints before touching code.
2. **Explore the data.** Look at distributions, missing values, class balance, and sample inputs.
3. **Build a baseline first.** A simple model gives you a score to beat and reveals data issues.
4. **Iterate in small steps.** Change one thing at a time and measure the impact.
5. **Validate properly.** A reliable local validation strategy is more important than a single public leaderboard score.

## Baseline checklist

- Load the data and verify shapes
- Create a minimal feature set
- Train a simple model (logistic regression, small MLP, pre-trained backbone)
- Submit or evaluate to establish a baseline
- Only then add complexity

> If you can't beat a simple baseline, a bigger model won't help.
